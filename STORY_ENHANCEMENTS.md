# Story Enhancements

## Goal

Transform the application from a data dashboard into an interactive data story.

The experience should guide users through a narrative:

1. Challenge assumptions
2. Reveal surprising insights
3. Explain the data
4. Help users take action

---

## Updated Story Flow

1. Hero
2. Quiz
3. Myth vs Reality
4. Day in the Life
5. Energy Breakdown
6. Habit Impact Calculator
7. Personalized Energy Plan
8. Final Takeaway

---

## New Section: Myth vs Reality

Place directly after the Quiz section.

Create 3-4 cards.

Example:

### Myth

Turning off lights saves the most energy.

### Reality

Heating and cooling consume significantly more energy.

Additional examples:

* Unplugging chargers has a huge impact.
* Electronics dominate energy bills.
* Laundry is the largest household energy expense.

Each card should be visually engaging and easy to scan.

---

## New Section: Day in the Life

Place before the Energy Breakdown section.

Tell the story of a typical day.

Timeline:

### 7:00 AM

Hot shower

Energy impact: High

### 8:00 AM

Lights and phone charging

Energy impact: Low

### 2:00 PM

Air conditioning running

Energy impact: Very High

### 6:00 PM

Laundry

Energy impact: Medium

### 8:00 PM

TV and electronics

Energy impact: Low

Show cumulative energy usage growing throughout the day.

End with:

"Most of today's energy came from temperature control and hot water."

---

## Energy Breakdown

Keep the existing chart.

Add a large insight callout:

"Heating, cooling, and hot water account for more than 60% of home energy use."

---

## Habit Calculator

Group actions into:

### High Impact

* Lower thermostat by 2°F

### Medium Impact

* Shorter showers
* Wash clothes cold

### Low Impact

* Turn off lights
* Unplug chargers

Explain why impact differs.

---

## Personalized Energy Plan

Improve storytelling.

Show:

* Dollars saved
* Energy saved
* CO₂ reduced
* Equivalent trees planted
* Equivalent miles not driven

---

## Final Takeaway

Create a visually strong ending section.

Headline:

"You don't need to change everything."

Supporting text:

"The most sustainable homes aren't the ones doing the most. They're the ones focusing on what matters most."

Include a clear call-to-action to start with one high-impact habit.
